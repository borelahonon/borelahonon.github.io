Research

Working Papers

Sovereign Credit Risk and the Tale of Two Inflations

This paper investigates how different types of inflation (demand-pull vs. cost-push) affect sovereign credit risk and bond yields. We provide empirical evidence on the differential impact of inflation sources on government borrowing costs.

Working Paper
Presented at: 12th Montreal Business School Joint-PhD Symposium

When Long-Run Trends Are Unknown: Bond Pricing Implications

with Guillaume Roussellet

This paper examines how uncertainty about long-run economic trends affects bond pricing and financial market behavior. We develop a theoretical framework that incorporates learning about persistent economic trends and analyze its implications for yield curve dynamics and risk premia.

Working Paper
Presented at: Federal Reserve Bank of New York, SCSE Congress, FRBC-FRBSF-JFE Conference, McGill University

A Macrofinance Perspective on the Twin Ds - Default and Depreciation Risks

This research explores the interaction between sovereign default risk and currency depreciation risk in emerging markets. We develop a unified framework that accounts for both risks and their impact on international capital flows and financial stability.

Working Paper
Presented at: NFA Annual Meetings, SFA Annual Meetings, EFMA Doctoral Seminar, IAFDS, ISAFE, CEA Annual Meetings, 19th CIREQ PhD Students' Conference, McGill University
